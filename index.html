The battle of Egadi Tags: Nature, Hystory

by Giuseppe Ferrante, August 2013

Local, born in Favignana Island, Sicily, Italy
Cala Rossa (Red Cove) is probably the most beautiful beach in Favignana. Since I was a child I can remember the sea water color ranging from blue, light blue and green...crystal clear. Many years later I could see picture of Caribbean, but Cala Rossa has nothing to envy. When is blowing the south wind of Scirocco, take a bike and get a ride from town. You'll feel the hot on your skin but you'll love to go there when the sea is dead and calm. Don't believe the people (I've read even on Wikipedia...) who said that the name "Red Cove" derives from bloodshed of the Second Punic War. That's not correct. I'll tell you the true story. On 10 March 241 BC, near Levanzo Island, just 2 miles ahead of Cala Rossa, a major naval battle was fought between the Roman and Carthaginian fleets. Two hundred Roman ships under the consul Gaius Lutatius Catulus faced a much larger Carthaginian fleet of 400 ships. The Carthaginians moved to the coast of Sicily. The Romans hided their fleet behind Levanzo island, waiting for the enemies passage. When the last Carthaginian ships was passed they attacked behind taking advantage of the NOrth-West wind and decisively defeated the larger Carthaginian fleet. The Romans sinking 120 Carthaginian vessels and taking 10,000 prisoners. The Romans took possession of the island under the terms of the treaty that ended the war. For sure, there were so many deads, but the name Red Cove has become from the red clay on this beautiful beach and not for the bloodshed.

Wherever you are, anyone can be your Cicero and you can be a Cicero for millions of people

Why would I want another web travel-oriented service? You're already using Foursquare, Tripadvisor, Virtualtourist and many others. These services are great and TellMeCicero is not trying to compete with them. Therefore, you'll likely want to use TellMeCicero among the services that you're using now, not instead.

TellMeCicero is based upon a very simple idea: wherever the place you're in, it would be nice to hear stories that bring to the foreground the informations which no one knows about. So:

1
Isn't about finding places (eg. Foursquare). Is about discovering something unexpected when in place.
2
Isn't about planning your travel (eg. Virtualtourist). Is about living your travel experience moment by moment.
3
Isn't about reviewing hotels or restaurants (eg. Tripadvisor). Is about conveying travel stories and personal feelings about things that haven't really seen the light of day.

TellMeCicero is both a web site and an innovative mobile app to bring the whole travel experience to a new level. TellMeCicero app provides augmented reality features and the infrastructure for people to "plant" in a location audio and text stories to be used by travelers and others. Unleash the power of augmented reality through three simple steps:

1
Check-in

Turn on GPS services on your device (smartphone, tablet and, pretty soon, Google Glass), launch TellMeCicero app and sign in.
2
Point and discover

Point your device around you and check for icons and graphic elements overlaying the screen.
3
Get stories and advices

Get along the way available audio and text stories provided by other travelers and locals, simply grabbing them from the screen.
Top

As a traveler, we know that you want to experience the present moment of your travel. So you can:

1
Record an audio story through TellMeCicero app and plant it in the place you're in by uploading to the TellMeCicero cloud. You can ask for an automatic text translation of your records via speech-to-text feature provided by TellMeCicero platform.
2
Share your stories and feelings about places, giving a valuable contribution to other travelers
3
Organize your multimedia travel memories (audio, post, pictures) through TellMeCicero web site, to compose your personal "memento travel journal"

As a local, we know that you are the best Cicero of your area. So you can:

1
Record and post stories about your birthplace or the place where you live.
2
Build alternative travel patterns about your place, organizing your contributions through TellMeCicero web site.
3
Share 'em all and make available to other people.

FAQ

What is TellMeCicero?
TellMeCicero is both a web site and an innovative mobile app which provides augmented reality features, to bring the whole travel experience to a new level.
Will it be free a service?
TellMeCicero will be 100% free for ever and ever
What is the Stack?
We are OSS believers. TellMeCicero will be built on top of the technologies that are freely available to us today.
Why support us?
Donating Bitcoins, or even simply spreading the word via Twitter or Facebook sharing is the way for you to help make this project a reality. I will definitely be needing help from people to build a MVP (Minimum Viable Product) for the project and manage the startup launch. Without your support and donations, there isn't really a way for us to make that happen. All of you who will contribute will be contacted to become early adopters before the official go live. A thing to keep in mind is that the rewards will be bragging rights recognition, a way for you to help make the project a reality.
Why Bitcoin payments?
A Paypal donation button is forthcoming, but we prefer Bitcoin payments. We believe Bitcoin is the currency of the future and you can consult this page to learn how to buy BitCoins in your country.
Why 2000 BTC?
While I can put together a prototype on my own, I certainly can't build entirely TellMeCicero web site and mobile app in any reasonable amount of time by myself. The more money we get, the larger the team I can put together to turn this stuff into a reality. The 2k BTC, after taxes and cost, will help us at least assemble a small but smart team to launch first release of the system.
Does more funding mean more stuff?
Sure! I can hire a bigger and more diverse team so that we can add support for more features and even cooler things than I've shown in the presentation (e.g. a tourist booklet generated with stories of the place you're planning to visit and much more)
How long will it take?
The tentative plan is to release a full beta within 4 months and have an official launch April of next year. As it stands currently, it will include all the things I've shown in the presentation, included the augmented reality mobile app, but should we continue to get more funding we can add even more.
Why should we believe you can deliver?
I've been programming professionally for more than 20 years, during which time I've worked on information retrieval, content, document and knowledge management, built websites and portals large and small, delivering content and services both in internet and intranet environment and released libraries and frameworks (one of these, Web@Work, a CMS entirely built in PL/SQL and completely embeddable into any Oracle DBMS, was recognized by Oracle as Award Winning partner solution in 2004). I have a hystory of building useful software and, as PMP, of managing large ICT projects as well as experience leading and shaping the future of a rapidly growing startup.
